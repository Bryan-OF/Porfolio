Audio Mix Project

For one of my exchange program courses at Edinburgh Napier University, I chose the Sound for Digital media course as part of my Digital Media coursework. The goal was to create a 3-minute soundtrack for a live-action or animated film by completely replacing the existing audio. The challenge was to contrast the new soundtrack with the original to give the film a different feel. This project aimed to develop and showcase skills in audio editing and sound design, using Reaper to enhance the film's auditory experience.

Soundtrack Creation:

Composed and mixed an entirely new soundtrack to replace the original audio, focusing on creating a unique auditory experience that transformed the feel of the selected film scene.

Audio Editing, Synchronization and Marking:

Utilized Reaper to edit, synchronize, and layer sound effects, dialogue, and music. This process involved meticulous attention to detail to ensure that the new audio matched the visual elements perfectly. Using Reaper, I could track how many audio effects was needed for my project. Using markers, I could track how many sounds effect each scene had, character movement and characters dialog was played during the full video.

Sound Effect Creation:

Halfway during the course, we were taught how to make sound effect form regular objects. Using bottle caps on metal for moving armor sounds to coconut shells to imitated horse walking. This method I used to create different sound in my project so I did not have to get them from a online source.

Video Editing:

For this project I chose an edit from the animated film Berserk: The Golden Age Arc trilogy for its high level of action and audio changes. The original edit of the scene used movie version of the adaptation, but I change it to the episodic anniversary version for its improved visuals.

Final Outcome:

The end result was a refined 3-minute audio mix that came close to the auditory experience of the selected film Edit. By replacing the original audio with a fitting but game recognizable soundtrack, I was able to provide a fresh perspective on the scene and enhance its overall impact. The project showcased my ability to manage intricate audio changes and deliver a polished and engaging final product.

This project was completed as part of my exchange program at Edinburgh Napier University, where I had the opportunity to deepen my skills in digital media design and engage with advanced audio editing techniques. The experience enriched my understanding of sound design and solidified my capabilities in producing high-quality audio work.
